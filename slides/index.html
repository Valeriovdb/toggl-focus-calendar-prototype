01 / Why this area

The calendar is where time intelligence breaks or becomes trustworthy.

Toggl Focus has the ingredients for time intelligence, but the current calendar experience does not make it clear enough what is planned, tracked, assigned, or report-ready.

Freelancers

Risk missed billable time and weaker profitability signals.

Embedded ICs

Risk weaker proof of work, scope control, and renewal evidence.

Color and state logic are hard to parse
Timer context can feel disconnected
The data-quality loop is not obvious
Planned work → Actual work → Logged time → Reportable insight
02 / Prioritization and prototype

I focused upstream: review, repair, confirm.

A better reports UI cannot fully solve the trust problem if the underlying time data is incomplete or unassigned.

Chosen focus

1 Calendar-based day review Highest leverage for recurring friction and data quality.
2 Timer usability Important, but Toggl is already strong here.
3 Reports Strategic, but downstream of data completeness.
Left out

Full reports redesign, mobile, onboarding, project setup, team capacity planning.

Prototype workflow

1 Review next Planning intelligence highlights what needs attention.
2 Assign project/task Suggested only when confidence is high.
3 Log activity Past calendar work becomes reportable time.
4 Update state Calendar and attention summary reflect better data.
03 / Tradeoffs, impact, measurement, AI

The prototype is intentionally narrow: enough to test the workflow logic, not a full production redesign.

Tradeoffs

  • Depth over breadth: one workflow
  • Speed over fidelity: simulated frontend state
  • Trust over automation: conservative autosuggest
  • Desktop first: review needs context

Expected impact

  • Fewer missed billable hours
  • Fewer unassigned entries
  • More trustworthy reports
  • Stronger week 2 / week 4 retention

Measurement

  • calendar_review_completed
  • % unlogged activities resolved
  • report_view_after_review
  • uncategorized_time_share

AI usage

  • Research synthesis
  • Case structure
  • Frontend prototyping
  • Overrode AI on scope, priority, and trust decisions
Make individual time data complete enough to trust before asking reports to do more.
