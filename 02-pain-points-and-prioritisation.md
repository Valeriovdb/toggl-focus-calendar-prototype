# Pain Points and Prioritisation

## Areas of Improvement Considered

- Onboarding personalization: help users choose a setup path based on whether they are a Freelancer or embedded IC.
- Project/client setup: make it easier to structure clients, projects, tasks, tags, rates, and billable settings.
- Time entry and timer usability: improve starting, stopping, editing, and recovering time entries.
- Calendar-based day review and time confirmation: help users review planned/calendar activity, assign it to project/task, confirm what was actually worked, and make it reportable.
- Reports and individual insights: translate tracked time into profitability, scope creep, utilization, client value, and planning decisions.

## Why the Selected Area Combines Multiple Workflows

The prioritisation exercise separates opportunities so they can be compared clearly, but in the product experience some workflows are naturally connected.

The selected opportunity combines:

- Calendar / timeline planning: the surface where users see what was supposed to happen.
- Day reconstruction / review: the workflow where users confirm what actually happened.
- Task / plan connection: the mechanism that turns activity into structured, reportable time data.

This overlap is intentional. The user problem is not isolated to one screen. Users lose trust when the loop breaks between planning work, doing work, logging work, and understanding work.

The selected opportunity is therefore framed as **calendar-based day review and time confirmation**.

## Prioritisation Criteria

Scoring scale: 1 = low, 5 = high.

- Persona pain severity: how painful and frequent is the problem for Freelancers and ICs?
- Strategic fit with time intelligence: does the area help turn time data into planning, insight, boundaries, or better decisions?
- Retention potential: would improving this create a repeated habit or reduce reasons to churn?
- Friction / usability impact: does it remove repeated manual effort, confusion, or recovery work?
- Data quality impact: does it improve the completeness, accuracy, or trustworthiness of time data?

## Prioritised Area

The highest-priority area is **calendar-based day review and time confirmation**.

This area scores highest because it addresses a recurring problem for both target personas: real work is fragmented, users forget to track perfectly, and incomplete time data weakens the value of reporting and planning. Improving this flow directly supports retention, reduces friction, improves usability, and increases trust in the data that powers time intelligence.

The solution uses the calendar as the surface, day review as the core workflow, and project/task linking as the data-quality mechanism.
